Colour Theory

Colour theory is vital when designing a website, you need your colours to be of a certain house style to keep your brand image cohesive. My main colour comes from the background image, I wanted to use a bold, bright image of the Toronto skyline during a warm summer sunset. These dark red and orange colours are to give off a warm, vibrant feel to the user. The shadow from the buildings makes the text more readable. As for my secondary colours I have decided to move forward with mainly black and white detailing as it gives it. A clean edge and allows for the background image to provide the colouring I want to be associated with JI media. In 2021, Hampton-Smith told CreativeBloq about the importance of colour wheels when seeing the relationship between colours on a screen.

Typography

When designing my website, the typography is something I thought deeply about. I wanted to have a font for my headers which were bold and professional looking. I needed to make sure the colour of the headings was also complimentary towards the vibrant colours used on background. I went for white as it gives a clean look and adds to the effect of the font. For my heading I used San-Serif style as I wanted my headings to look bolder. I learned the difference between them (Macon, 2021) and made sure that I used Serif for my sub text as it easier to read. By having my text in serif, it allows users who may struggle reading lots of small text.

Accessibility

Accessibility is something which I wanted to expand on my website. Accessibility is key in making sure that any website is ready to be used by any person who wishes to enjoy. One of the main way’s websites deal with accessibility through images is by including Alt text. Alt text will basically describe the image to a person who is blind who otherwise wouldn’t be able to see the image – it allows for you to optimise your websites audience. According to The Whole Brain Group “If you do not have an accessible website, you are missing out on about 15 – 20% of U.S” (2012). This statistic is a reality to how important it is to make your accessibility work for everyone. I tried to include a format which was easy to navigate.
